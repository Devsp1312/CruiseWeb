Understanding MSC Dress Codes üìã

Casual: Shorts, t-shirts, polos. Swimwear by pool/beach ONLY (not in formal dining).

Polished: Collared shirts (polos, button-downs), tailored trousers/chinos, smart shorts. No casual shorts, flip-flops, tank tops.

Elegant: Dark suit with shirt & tie, or smart blazer with dress pants/chinos & tie. Tuxedo optional. No t-shirts, shorts, flip-flops in main dining.

üçîüçπ Food & Drinks Guide

Navigating dining on the MSC Seascape, including your notes on what to try and drink packages.

Dining Venues & Tips üçΩÔ∏è

  • Marketplace Buffet: Main buffet, includes vegan section. Open 24/7 (verify hours for full service). Can be crowded at peak times; try going early/late. Pizza is good. Make-your-own sandwiches/bowls (Qdoba style). Fresh fruit. üí°Avoid dinner buffet if possible, per user notes.
  • Main Dining Rooms (4 total): Menu changes daily. Same waiter for all 7 days. QR code for drink orders (sometimes buggy). Late dining: 7:45 PM - 9:45 PM. Ask if you can dine together (link bookings). Indian food night (Aloo Gobi, Naan).
  • Hola! Tacos & Cantina: Specialty dining, try to make a reservation. (Menu photos in user notes)
  • Room Service: Free delivery with Fantastica Experience. $4 delivery for breakfast in room (worth it on early excursion days).
  • Ice Cream & Desserts: Free ice cream at lunch/dinner. Gelato costs extra. Chocolate bar available. Lots of desserts - try them all! Specific mentions: Caribbean Crumble (Day 6), Tiramisu, Melting Cream Cake (like cheesecake but better). Cheesecake available daily.
  • General Food Notes: Food can be hit-or-miss. Roasted Vegetable Lasagna, Pennette Pasta mentioned as good.
